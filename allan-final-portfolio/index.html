    The Beginning

    I'm Allan Paulraj V — a 19-year-old AI & ML engineering student at Karunya Institute of Technology and Sciences, Coimbatore. It started with a broken laptop and a YouTube tutorial at 2AM. I wanted to fix something small, and ended up discovering I could build something entirely my own. That feeling never left.

    Why AI & Full Stack

    I chose AI & ML because it's the one field where the code thinks. Training a model that learns patterns from data — and then actually getting it right — felt like teaching something to see. I was hooked.

    At Karunya, I built NeuroAI — a brain MRI classifier detecting four tumor classes using EfficientNetB0 paired with Mistral 7B LLM for diagnostic report generation. Then came SmartPark — real-time multi-floor parking management with dynamic pricing and GST billing. Then IndPopHub — a comprehensive India population intelligence dashboard.

    "I don't want to just write code.
    I want to build things that matter."
    Building Full Products

    I'm not a specialist who hides in one corner. I build the whole stack — backend logic with Node.js and FastAPI, intelligent frontends in React, ML pipelines in TensorFlow, and I deploy them. Live. On Vercel, Firebase, or wherever the problem demands.

    I led media production for Google Developer Group on campus. I interned at Fuzionest, Lysa Solutions, and Oasis Infobyte — each time delivering under real deadlines. I care about the whole product — architecture, interface, performance, and the user clicking a button at 3AM.

    Beyond the Screen

    I shoot photos. I travel when I can. I believe storytelling — whether through a camera or a codebase — is the same discipline: make the invisible, visible. I cycle in the early morning. I train consistently. I read obsessively.

    The same standard I hold for a pull request, I hold for everything else. Precision isn't a work mode. It's a habit.

    Right Now

    Second year. Building scalable AI-powered digital experiences. Looking for an internship where the problems are real and the bar is high.

    If you're reading this, you're probably deciding whether I'm worth reaching out to. The answer is yes — let's build something worth shipping.
